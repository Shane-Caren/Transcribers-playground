Introduction to transcribing


Transcription is the process of turning audio files into text files. Generally a Transcriber will do this using touch typing or ten finger typing.

Doing this allows the transcriber to focus on the audio while watching the text being typed for errors instead of looking at the keyboard.

Accuracy is the most important part of transcribing but speed is needed to make any form of income off the practice.

The minimum speed to be profitable is 60 words per minute.

Practicing will quickly improve most peoples' speed, so any transcriber is encouraged to practice as often as they can for at least 10 - 20 minutes a day
