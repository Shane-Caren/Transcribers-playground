Introduction to transcribing


Transcription is the process of turning audio files into text files. Generally a Transcriber will do this using touch typing or ten finger typing.

Doing this allows the transcriber to focus on the audio while watching the text being typed for errors instead of looking at the keyboard.

Accuracy is the most important part of transcribing but speed is needed to make any form of income off the practice.

The minimum speed to be profitable is 60 words per minute.

Practicing will quickly improve most peoples' speed, so any transcriber is encouraged to practice as often as they can for at least 10 - 20 minutes a day

Helpful Equipment


To begin as a transcriber you only need a working computer, headphones and a working keyboard but many people use additional equipment to increase their efficiency.

Many people use an ergonomic keyboard to avoid repetitive stress injuries to the wrist, these keyboards make sure to take the strain off the wrist as you type.

There is also an option of a split keyboard which can help to get used to finger placement while typing.

You can get a foot pedal to control the audio so you can pause and rewind without taking your hands away from the keyboard or searching for the correct key to pause things and find where you were in the audio. Most pedals can be set to rewind by set increments.
